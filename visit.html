What to do

Granada is most famous for the stunning Moorish palace of Alhambra, drawing visitors from all over the globe because of its breathtaking beauty and fascinating history.
Doing a tour here can take around half a day, but aside from that, there are plenty of other awesome things to do in Granada, Spain. Add the city to your Andalusia road trip and spend a few days here relaxing, wandering the beautiful streets, and soaking up the chilled way of life in this enchanting city.

  1. Alhambra

    The Alhambra is a World Herigate Site and the most visited monument in Spain, and obviously, the best place to see in Granada.
    The monumental combination of the Alhambra, where the Nasrid kings and the entire court who lived there between 12th and 15th centuries, is made up of the Alcazaba, the Palaces, the Generalife gardens and infinity of patios, fountains and impressive displays of Moorish art.
    Also in its golden age its interior hid mosques, workshops, schools,gardens, fountains, love, heartbreak, houses, kittens, business, fun and suffering. Centuries have passed since the reconquest ended Arab hegemon and not even Napoleon was able to end it; it seems that Spanish solider did not want obey his order to demolish it with explosives. Goodness! Without a doubt, it is the great jewel to see in Granada.

  3. have a walk through Carrera del darro

    The Darro River is a tributary of the Genil that flows through Granada and crosses the center of the city between the Albaicin hill and the hill of the Alhambra. Of the thirteen bridges that used to pass through Granada, the Darro has maintained only four. Two of them are located on the famous Carrera del Darro, the Cabrera and Espinosa, and are made of brick and stonework.
